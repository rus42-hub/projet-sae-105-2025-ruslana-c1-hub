Sofia Kovalevskaya —
a forgotten pioneer

A woman ahead of her time

She was not yet thirty when she solved equations that even the greatest scientists deemed impossible.
But in those days, it wasn’t the result that shocked people most — it was the fact that it came from a woman.

“The Matilda Effect” refers to the systematic erasure of women from the history of science. Long before the term existed, Sofia was already a victim of it. Her work was first attributed to male colleagues, her discoveries downplayed, her efforts reduced to the “assistance” of a professor.

The price of genius

To prove she was worthy, she had to leave her country, marry without love, and face contempt and loneliness. Behind the differential equations was a silent struggle — that of a woman who refused to be a man’s “appendage.”

Legacy

Even today, Sofia’s story echoes that of many other women researchers. Their names fade away, their discoveries survive — anonymously. But every time we speak of the Matilda Effect, somewhere, Sofia’s voice is heard again.
