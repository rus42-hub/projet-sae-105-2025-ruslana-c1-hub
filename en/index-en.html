Sofia Kovalevskaya

mathematician, pioneer

Since Antiquity, women were destined to stay at home.
They were expected to be gentle, patient, obedient.

But one day, they appeared — those who refused that fate.
Those who read when everyone else was asleep.
Those who knocked on the closed doors of science.

Among them — Sofia Kovalevskaya.
The first woman professor of mathematics in Europe.

A voice that was silenced for far too long.

The “Matilda Effect” refers to the phenomenon in which the discoveries of women scientists were attributed to their male colleagues — a deeper way of denying their place and contribution in the history of science.
